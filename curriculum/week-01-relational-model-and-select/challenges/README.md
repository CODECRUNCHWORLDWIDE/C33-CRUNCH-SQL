# Week 1 — Challenges

Two open-ended problems. Unlike the exercises, these have **no single right answer** — the skill is judgment, not recall. Do them after the three exercises.

1. **[Challenge 1 — Ambiguous business questions](challenge-01-ambiguous-business-questions.md)** — a manager asks vague questions in plain English; you decide what they *precisely* mean and defend the `WHERE` clause you wrote. *(~60 min.)*
2. **[Challenge 2 — NULL gotchas](challenge-02-null-gotchas.md)** — a set of queries that look correct and return the wrong rows because of `NULL`. Find each bug, explain it, and fix it. *(~60 min.)*

## How these are judged

There's no answer key with row counts. Instead, each challenge tells you what a *strong* submission looks like. You're being graded on:

- **Correctness of reasoning** — can you justify why your query answers the question?
- **Explicit assumptions** — did you write down the interpretation you chose when the question was ambiguous?
- **Awareness of `NULL`** — did you account for missing values instead of being surprised by them?

Keep your work in a `challenge-01.md` / `challenge-02.md` with your queries **and** your written reasoning. The reasoning is the point.
