# Week 5 — Challenges

Two open-ended problems. Unlike the exercises, these have **no single right answer** — they test whether you can *decide* and *defend a decision* about concurrency, which is the actual senior-engineer skill this week builds toward.

| # | Challenge | What it tests | Time |
|--:|-----------|---------------|-----:|
| 1 | [challenge-01-pick-the-isolation-level.md](./challenge-01-pick-the-isolation-level.md) | Choosing an isolation level per scenario and justifying the trade-off | ~1h |
| 2 | [challenge-02-fix-a-lost-update.md](./challenge-02-fix-a-lost-update.md) | Diagnosing a lost-update bug and fixing it three different ways | ~1h |

## How these are judged

There's no autograder. Judge yourself (or have a peer judge you) against the rubric at the bottom of each file. The bar is not "did you find *an* answer" — it's **"can you explain the trade-off you made and name what you gave up."** A choice you can't justify is worth nothing here, even if it happens to be correct; a defensible choice with a clear-eyed account of its costs is full marks.

Write your reasoning in a `writeup.md` per challenge and commit under `c33-week-05/challenge-0N/`.
